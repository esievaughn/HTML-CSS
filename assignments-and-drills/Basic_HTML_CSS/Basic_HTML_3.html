STELLER'S JAY & BLUE JAYS

Steller's Jay are a resident in evergreen forests in the western United States.
Steller's Jay are related to the Blue Jay, but have blue and black coloring
with a black crested feathered head.

Steller's Jay and Blue Jays are the only jays that use mud to build their nests

Unlike other species of birds where males and females express different colors,
male and female Blue Jays look alike.This is called sexual monomorphism.
Blue Jays are apart of the Corvidae family of birds(including crows, ravens, and magpies),
and are considered especially intelligent with tool making capabilities and curiosity.
